Questions every product owner should answer annually

Rather than discussing dozens of features, I’d have each offering answer the same questions:

Question	Why it matters
Who are our customers?	Clarifies the audience and avoids building for “everyone.”
How do we measure adoption?	Establishes objective growth metrics.
What business outcome do we enable?	Connects the platform to firm value rather than technology.
Why should the firm continue investing in this offering?	Forces a clear articulation of strategic value.
What happens if we stop investing?	Distinguishes foundational services from experimental ones.
Should we Grow, Sustain, Maintain, or Evaluate this offering over the next 12 months?	Drives portfolio-level prioritization.

It shifts the conversation from “what features should we build next?” to “which platforms are strategic, which are mature, and where should our limited engineering capacity go?” That’s a much healthier discussion for an organization with a broad set of responsibilities and a finite team.
